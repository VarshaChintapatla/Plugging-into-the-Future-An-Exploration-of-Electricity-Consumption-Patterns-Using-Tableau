Plugging into the Future: An Exploration of Electricity Consumption Patterns

India is the third largest producer of electricity in the world. During the fiscal year (FY) 2019–20, the total electricity generation in the country was 1,598 TWh, of which 1,383.5 TWh generated by utilities. The gross electricity consumption per capita in FY2019 was 1,208 kWh.

About

This project analyzes electricity consumption patterns across various states in India to understand regional demand, usage trends, and energy distribution. As India continues to grow economically, electricity plays a crucial role in industrial development, infrastructure expansion, and household consumption.

  • State-wise comparison of electricity consumption levels.
  • Identification of highest and lowest electricity consuming states.
  • Interactive data visualization using Tableau dashboards.

Through detailed data analysis and visualization techniques, this project provides meaningful insights into electricity demand growth and regional consumption disparities. The findings help in understanding energy usage patterns and support better planning for sustainable energy management and policy decisions.

Why Electricity Consumption Analysis is Important for Sustainable Growth

Electricity plays a vital role in economic development, industrial expansion, and improving living standards. Understanding consumption patterns helps governments and policymakers plan efficient energy distribution and ensure sustainable resource management.

01 Why analyze state-wise electricity consumption?

State-wise analysis helps identify regions with high and low electricity usage. It supports better allocation of resources and improves infrastructure planning based on regional demand.

02 How does data visualization improve understanding?

Interactive dashboards and visual charts make complex electricity data easier to interpret. They allow users to compare trends, detect patterns, and draw meaningful insights efficiently.

03 What are the key outcomes of this project?

The project identifies Maharashtra as the highest electricity consumer, Gujarat as the second highest, and Sikkim as the lowest. These insights highlight regional differences in energy demand across India.

Project Insights

Key findings and analytical observations from electricity consumption data

State-Level Consumption Trends

State Analysis

Maharashtra recorded the highest electricity consumption, followed by Gujarat. Sikkim showed the lowest usage. This reflects differences in industrial development and urbanization across states.

Impact of COVID-19 Lockdown

Time-Based Analysis

Electricity demand dropped significantly during lockdown periods due to reduced industrial and commercial activities. This highlights the strong connection between economic activity and energy consumption.

Regional & Seasonal Patterns

Regional Analysis

Southern and Western regions show higher electricity demand compared to other regions. Monthly and quarterly trends indicate seasonal peaks, especially during summer months.
